Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

NSU follows a letter grade system. Each letter grade corresponds to a grade point. For example, 'A' is 4.0, 'A-' is 3.7, 'B+' is 3.3, and so on. The final CGPA is a weighted average of these grade points based on the credit hours of the courses.

The CGPA is calculated by dividing the total quality points by the total number of credits completed. Quality points for a course are calculated by multiplying the grade point achieved in that course by the number of credits for that course. This calculator automates that entire process for you.

While a "good" CGPA is subjective and depends on your academic and career goals, a CGPA of 3.5 or above is generally considered excellent. A CGPA above 3.0 is typically required for many scholarships and postgraduate opportunities.
